Section 1

Executive Summary

ZjazdTutaj.pl is a Polish online car-selling marketplace that connects private vehicle sellers directly with a network of verified franchise and independent dealers. Sellers receive competitive, data-driven offers without visiting a forecourt. Dealers gain access to a curated, high-quality supply of pre-owned vehicles.

This document defines the complete product scope, feature set, technical requirements, and recommended third-party integrations required for a development partner to design, build, test, and launch the platform. It covers five core product areas:

  • Public-facing marketing website
  • Seller account portal (the seller journey from valuation to sale)
  • Dealer account portal (the buyer journey from browsing to bidding and acquisition)
  • Dealer auction engine (sealed-bid, time-limited auction mechanics)
  • Internal backoffice (admin and operations tooling)

The platform must be built to production quality, comply with Polish and EU law (GDPR, Consumer Rights Act, AML), and integrate with Polish-market-specific data sources such as CEPiK, KRS, GUS, and Polish payment providers.

Section 2

Product Overview & Business Model

2.1 How the platform works

The core flow is a reverse marketplace: sellers create listings, dealers compete, and sellers accept the best offer.

  • Seller registers, submits vehicle details and documentation.
  • Platform validates the listing using automated and manual checks (CEPiK lookup, photo review, mileage cross-reference).
  • Approved listings enter the dealer auction pool for a fixed auction window (default 24 hours).
  • Registered dealers browse listings, review vehicle reports, and place sealed bids.
  • At auction close, the seller reviews all bids and accepts one.
  • The accepting dealer arranges vehicle collection. Payment is made directly between dealer and seller.
  • Platform invoices the dealer a success fee upon confirmed sale.

2.2 Revenue model

  • Free for sellers — zero cost, zero commission.
  • Dealers pay a monthly subscription to access auctions.
  • Dealers pay a per-vehicle success fee on completed purchases.
  • Optional premium listing features for sellers (sponsored placement, enhanced photos).

2.3 Target market

Poland is the largest used-car market in Central and Eastern Europe, with approximately 2 million private vehicle transactions per year. The platform initially targets the top 20 Polish cities, with national rollout in phase 2. Primary seller demographic: 25–55-year-old private individuals. Primary dealer demographic: licensed Polish car dealers (komisanci and autoryzowane stacje dealerskie).

Section 3

Technical Architecture

3.1 Frontend

  • Framework: Next.js 14+ (React, App Router, SSR/SSG for SEO-critical pages)
  • Language: TypeScript throughout
  • Styling: Tailwind CSS with a custom design system
  • State: Zustand (global) + React Query (server state)
  • Mobile: Responsive-first web. Native apps (React Native) in Phase 2.
  • i18n: next-intl, Polish as primary locale

3.2 Backend

  • Runtime: Node.js with NestJS (REST + WebSocket)
  • Database: PostgreSQL (primary), Redis (caching, auction state, sessions)
  • Storage: AWS S3 (vehicle photos, documents)
  • Search: Elasticsearch or Meilisearch
  • Queue: BullMQ on Redis (async jobs)
  • Auth: JWT + refresh tokens. OAuth2 for social login.

3.3 Infrastructure

  • Cloud: AWS or GCP — EU regions only (Warsaw/Frankfurt for GDPR)
  • Containers: Docker + Kubernetes (EKS or GKE)
  • CI/CD: GitHub Actions with automated tests and staged deployment
  • Monitoring: Datadog or Grafana stack
  • CDN: Cloudflare (static assets, DDoS protection)

3.4 Security requirements

  • All data in transit: TLS 1.3 minimum
  • All data at rest: AES-256 encryption
  • GDPR Article 25 — privacy by design and by default
  • OWASP Top 10 compliance; penetration testing before launch
  • Role-based access control (RBAC) for all authenticated routes
  • Rate limiting on all public-facing APIs
  • Audit logging for all admin actions and sensitive data access
  • PCI-DSS considerations — use hosted payment pages only

Section 7

Dealer Auction Engine

Critical system: The auction engine must be reliable, fair, tamper-resistant, and capable of handling 500+ concurrent auctions. Zero bid loss tolerance.

7.1 Auction mechanics

  • Type: Sealed-bid first-price — dealers see bid count but not amounts
  • Window: Configurable per-listing — 12h, 24h, or 48h. Default 24h.
  • Start time: Fixed daily batch at 09:00 CET for consistent dealer patterns
  • Reserve price: Optional, set by seller or admin, never shown to dealers
  • Minimum bid increment: 500 PLN
  • Revision: Upward only, max 3 revisions per dealer per listing
  • Sniping prevention: Late bid in final 5 min extends auction by 5 min. Max total extension: 30 min.
  • Zero bids: Seller can extend by 24h once, relist, or withdraw

7.2 Bid integrity and security

  • All bids cryptographically signed with server-side timestamp — cannot be altered post-submission
  • Session verified server-side on every bid action
  • Rate limiting: 10 bid actions per minute per dealer
  • Immutable audit trail: timestamp, dealer ID, listing ID, amount, IP address
  • Fraud detection: anomaly alerts for unusual bidding patterns
  • Mid-auction dealer suspension by ops (non-disruptive to other bids)
  • Idempotent bid submission API

7.3 Real-time UI P2

  • WebSocket live updates: countdown, bid count, own bid status
  • Graceful fallback to 30-second polling
  • Two-step bid confirmation — review screen then confirm button
  • Outbid push notification and in-app alert
  • 1-hour closing alert via push and SMS
  • Full bidding functionality on mobile browser

Section 11

Legal & Compliance

Mandatory: Legal review by a qualified Polish legal firm is required before launch. This document does not constitute legal advice.

11.1 GDPR / RODO

  • Privacy policy in Polish, approved by legal counsel, with lawful basis for each processing activity
  • Cookie consent banner — granular consent, no pre-ticked boxes, compliant with UODO guidelines
  • Data subject rights: access (export), rectification, erasure, portability, objection — all automated or semi-automated in backoffice
  • Data retention: seller PII deleted or anonymised 2 years after last activity
  • DPA with all third-party processors (Stripe, SendGrid, Google, etc.)
  • Data breach procedure: 72-hour notification to UODO. Internal incident response plan required.
  • Assess DPO requirement under GDPR Article 37

11.2 Polish commercial law

  • Ustawa o prawach konsumenta: Applicable to seller-to-dealer transactions. Dealer always professional; seller may be consumer.
  • Ustawa o świadczeniu usług drogą elektroniczną: Electronic Services Act. Mandatory information disclosures in T&Cs.
  • PCC-3 tax: 2% civil law transaction tax on vehicle sales. Platform must inform sellers (informational only).
  • VAT: Invoice generation must support VAT invoices (faktura VAT) for dealer purchases.
  • AML (Ustawa AML): If escrow or payment facilitation is introduced, full AML compliance required. Legal review mandatory.

11.3 Sale agreement

  • Auto-generated PDF in Polish — legal template approved by counsel
  • Must contain: full party details, vehicle description (VIN, reg, make, model, year, mileage), agreed price in PLN, payment terms, seller's statement of right to sell, signature fields
  • Clear disclaimer: platform provides template as convenience only — not a legal service
